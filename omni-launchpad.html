How we read the scores

"Is 65 a good score?" depends on what the rest of the leaderboard looks like. Instead of inventing thresholds, every score below is anchored to four observable points from the actual leaderboard data.

The four anchors
Every benchmark has the same four reference points. Where Nemotron sits relative to them tells you what its score means.
Random floor
The score you get from guessing. For 4-way multiple choice that's 25%. Anything below this is broken.
Field median
The middle of the leaderboard. Below it, half the field is better than you. The de facto "usable" floor.
Top cluster
The top 5 entries grouped together. Inside this band, models are effectively interchangeable. The de facto "production" line.
SOTA
#1 on the leaderboard. The current ceiling. Often Nemotron — but not always.

Field medians are approximated from the visible leaderboard distribution (~30-40 models per benchmark). Top-cluster boundary is the score of the 5th-ranked model on each leaderboard.

Where we don't compete (and why it matters)

The five benchmarks above are the ones NVIDIA picked for the launch — surfaces where Nemotron wins or places. But there's a longer list of multimodal and reasoning benchmarks where Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-4o, Gemini 3 Pro, and DeepSeek V4 all report scores, and Nemotron 3 Nano Omni doesn't appear at all. Some of those absences are strategic blind spots NVIDIA should close. Others belong to a different model class and can be ignored.

Gaps to close — multimodal staples we should publish on

MMMU
Massive Multi-discipline Multimodal Understanding. College-level questions across 30 subjects with figures, charts, and diagrams. Every serious VLM publishes here. Frontier scores cluster 65-78. Not appearing on MMMU is the single biggest credibility gap.
MathVista
Visual mathematical reasoning over geometry, function plots, and scientific figures. Frontier 65-78. Owning STEM developer mindshare requires a competitive score here.
MMBench · AI2D · ChartQA · DocVQA
The classic multimodal staples: general VL, science diagrams, chart understanding, document VQA. ChartQA scores particularly matter for enterprise developer adoption (BI dashboards, finance docs, analyst reports).
VideoMME
The standard general-video benchmark. Daily-Omni and WorldSense are A/V-specific; VideoMME is video-first. Frontier 65-78. Any pure-video use case will be evaluated against this leaderboard.
Chatbot Arena Vision
Human-preference Elo leaderboard for VLMs. The only place where "do users actually prefer this model" gets measured. NVIDIA invisible here = NVIDIA invisible in the "what should I pick" conversation developers are having today.
Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index
Aggregator score everyone in the AI press cites. Not being on it means NVIDIA models don't show up in the canonical "compare these LLMs" tables that journalists, analysts, and procurement teams reference.

Gaps to investigate — audio benchmarks beyond VoiceBench

MMAU
Massive Multitask Audio Understanding. Tests audio comprehension beyond voice (music, environmental sounds, speech in noise). Given Nemotron Omni's full audio modality, we should publish here.
AIR-Bench
Audio Instruction Reasoning. Complements VoiceBench (which is voice-input only) with general audio reasoning. Another natural surface for the Omni architecture.

Gaps to ignore — different model class, not our lane

SWE-bench Verified · TAU-bench · LiveCodeBench
Coding-agent benchmarks. Opus 4.7 dominates (70+ on SWE-bench Verified). Nemotron Omni is a multimodal model, not a coding agent. Different category, different model class. But this is where the "AI coder" mindshare lives — long-term, NVIDIA needs a coding-agent answer (separate model, separate benchmark surface).
GPQA Diamond · AIME 2025 · ARC-AGI 2 · MATH-500
Pure-reasoning benchmarks. Opus 4.7, DeepSeek R2, GPT-5 territory. Reasoning-tuned models, not multimodal models. Different lane — these are also a different model class from Omni.
OSWorld · WebArena
Computer-use and web-agent benchmarks. Anthropic and Google territory. Different model class entirely.
The honest read: Nemotron Omni is benchmarked on the surfaces it wins on. That's smart launch strategy, not deception — every model does this. The gaps to close are the multimodal staples (MMMU, MathVista, VideoMME, ChartQA) where the field is standardized and we'd benefit from being in the conversation, even at mid-pack. The gaps to ignore are the agentic/reasoning benchmarks that belong to a different model class. Frontier scores in this section are directional from public knowledge — verify on the live leaderboards before quoting externally.

Concrete use cases · where Nemotron Omni actually wins

Real examples, not categories. For each: what a small-to-mid company is doing today, what Nemotron Omni replaces, the rough economics, and the specific benchmark sub-score that proves the model can handle it.

Voice · Customer support agent
50-person SaaS replaces their Five9 voice IVR with self-hosted Nemotron

Today: Five9 voice IVR handles tier-1 calls (password resets, billing questions, basic troubleshooting). ASR is decent, but the response layer is canned (FAQ matching, not reasoning). ~$40K/year in licensing plus per-minute fees. Customers complain about getting stuck in loops.

With Nemotron Omni: Speech-in, structured response out, with safety refusal for off-topic requests. Self-hosted on one Lambda Labs H100 = ~$2,500/month amortized. Latency ~600ms (single forward pass) vs. cascaded ASR+LLM at ~1,500ms. Owns its own weights — no data leaves the company.

Why this is real: VoiceBench overall 89.4 (#1, SOTA) with sub-scores SD-QA 71.4 (spoken-dialog QA), IFEval 88.7 (instruction following), AdvBench 100 (safety refusal). The exact skill set a support agent needs.

Voice · Medical scribe
Primary-care clinic transcribes patient visits with a HIPAA-clean voice agent

Today: Doctors dictate to a third-party medical-scribe service at $7-12 per transcript. 25-second average response back, structured into SOAP notes. HIPAA business associate agreements with every vendor. Patient data flows to three external companies.

With Nemotron Omni: Doctor speaks; the model transcribes and structures the note in real time on a clinic-owned server. Safety refusal on hallucinated diagnoses (per FDA guidance for clinical decision support). HIPAA-clean by construction — no patient data ever leaves the clinic's network.

Why this is real: VoiceBench MMSU (Massive Multitask Spoken Understanding) 82.3 + IFEval 88.7. The model reliably follows structured-output instructions from voice input. The compliance story is the deciding factor — open weights are the only path to a fully on-prem voice-LLM.

Documents · Invoice processing at scale
200-person company replaces their BPO invoice-processing contract

Today: 5,000 supplier invoices per month routed to an Indian BPO for field extraction (vendor name, PO number, line items, amounts, due dates, tax). $0.50/invoice = $30K/year. 24-48 hour turnaround. Errors require manual rework.

With Nemotron Omni: Invoice PDF → Nemotron → structured JSON, end-to-end in one model. Self-hosted H100, batched at 100+ invoices/hour. ~$400/month amortized. Sub-second per invoice. Real-time AP dashboard becomes possible.

Why this is real: OCRBench v2 Extraction sub-score 93.3 — the single best score on the entire OCRBench v2 leaderboard, across any sub-task. Nemotron is literally the best model in the world at pulling structured fields out of documents. Not "competitive." Best.

Documents · Contract field extraction
Legal-tech startup ingests 100-page contracts without an Anthropic API bill

Today: PDF → AWS Textract OCR → Anthropic API for clause extraction. Two-stage pipeline; Anthropic costs scale with contract length (8K-token average context). At 500 contracts/month, ~$3-4K in API spend. The Textract step adds another $0.05/page.

With Nemotron Omni: PDF → Nemotron Omni (one model, end-to-end). Extracts parties, dates, indemnification clauses, governing law, termination terms. Open weights = compliance audit trail (no third-party data processor in the chain — a real concern for legal-tech buyers).

Why this is real: OCRBench v2 Extraction 93.3 + Understanding 90.4 (industry-wide). Multi-page reasoning is weaker (see MMLongBench caveat above), so this works well on single-document clause extraction but should not be trusted with cross-contract portfolio analysis without human review.

A/V · Meeting recap with screen-share context
Zoom-clone generates meeting recaps that include "and then Sarah pulled up the slide"

Today: Otter.ai / Fireflies / Gong generate recaps from audio only. They miss screen-share events entirely. "She mentioned Q3 numbers" with no link to the chart that was on screen at that moment.

With Nemotron Omni: Audio + video tracks fed in together. Recap includes "the customer asked about pricing at 14:30, and the enterprise-tier slide was on screen at that moment." Reps can replay the exact 30-second segment that matched a buyer question. Sales coaching becomes scalable.

Why this is real: Daily-Omni overall 74.5 (#1, SOTA) with Comparison sub-task 83.2 ("which event happened first?") and Context Understanding strong. Designed for exactly this audio-visual event-correlation pattern.

A/V · Lecture-to-study-guide
University LMS auto-generates timestamped study guides from recorded lectures

Today: Students slog through 90-minute recorded lectures. Some LMS platforms auto-generate transcripts but no structure — no chapter boundaries, no slide mapping, no "key concept" highlighting. Watch-completion rates are below 30%.

With Nemotron Omni: Lecture A/V → structured study guide. Chapter boundaries (audio topic shift + slide change). Key statements highlighted. "Watch this 45-second segment for the proof of theorem X" navigation. Quiz questions auto-generated from spoken content tied to slide content.

Why this is real: Daily-Omni Event Sequence and Context Understanding sub-skills cover this directly. The model can identify when the topic shifts (audio + visual signals together) and tie spoken statements to slide content at specific timestamps.

The narrative for marketing

The pitch

For most multimodal tasks, the frontier-tax is no longer worth paying.

Nemotron 3 Nano Omni is #1 or top-2 on three of five public multimodal benchmarks — VoiceBench (#1), Daily-Omni (#1), OCRBench v2 (#2, ahead of Gemini 3 Pro Preview). It does this with 3 billion active parameters in an open-weight model that fits on a single GPU.

The story isn't "Nemotron beats everything." The story is: the tasks where open models work are now the majority of multimodal tasks. Voice assistants, document extraction, everyday A/V reasoning — all production-ready, all open. The remaining frontier-only tasks (rich video understanding, multi-page PDF reasoning, visual spotting) are a shrinking, specific list. And the unsolved tasks (table math, sports video, precise A/V alignment) aren't a model-selection problem — no amount of API budget fixes them today.

The developer outcome: if you're building voice agents, doc parsers, video Q&A, or A/V indexing — pull Nemotron Omni today. You'll ship faster, pay less, and own your weights. If you need rich-domain video or long-doc reasoning, you'll pay frontier prices anyway. Be honest about the line.
