The narrative for marketing

The pitch

For most multimodal tasks, the frontier-tax is no longer worth paying.

Nemotron 3 Nano Omni is #1 or top-2 on three of five public multimodal benchmarks — VoiceBench (#1), Daily-Omni (#1), OCRBench v2 (#2, ahead of Gemini 3 Pro Preview). It does this with 3 billion active parameters in an open-weight model that fits on a single GPU.

The story isn't "Nemotron beats everything." The story is: the tasks where open models work are now the majority of multimodal tasks. Voice assistants, document extraction, everyday A/V reasoning — all production-ready, all open. The remaining frontier-only tasks (rich video understanding, multi-page PDF reasoning, visual spotting) are a shrinking, specific list. And the unsolved tasks (table math, sports video, precise A/V alignment) aren't a model-selection problem — no amount of API budget fixes them today.

The developer outcome: if you're building voice agents, doc parsers, video Q&A, or A/V indexing — pull Nemotron Omni today. You'll ship faster, pay less, and own your weights. If you need rich-domain video or long-doc reasoning, you'll pay frontier prices anyway. Be honest about the line.
