§ 3 · The four root causes

Awareness is the symptom. These are the disease.

Synthesized from developer forum posts (r/LocalLLaMA, HN, OpenRouter discussions), the open llama.cpp issue tracker, license analysis, and the model release pattern.

01
License friction — not actually open
NVIDIA Open Model License vs Apache 2.0 / MIT

Nemotron ships under the NVIDIA Open Model License, not Apache 2.0 (Qwen) or MIT (DeepSeek). Legal analysis flags ambiguous future revision rights, Delaware governing law, and Santa Clara exclusive jurisdiction — real burdens for non-US enterprise adopters. Qwen's 90,000+ enterprise adopters chose Apache 2.0 explicitly.

"The uncertainty that license conditions may be revised in the future is a risk that cannot be ignored… reports describing Nemotron as 'open source' are misleading." — Shuji Sado, license analysis, Dec 2025
02
Mamba-hybrid architecture breaks tooling
llama.cpp · MLX · vLLM · SGLang · Ollama support gaps

Nemotron-H and the new Nemotron 3 family are Mamba-Transformer hybrids. As of May 2026, llama.cpp issue #18064 — "Feature Request: Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B" — is still open. The most popular community inference engine doesn't support our flagship model family. That means Ollama, LM Studio, and every llama.cpp-derived tool effectively don't either.

"The hybrid architecture forces the ecosystem to evolve — frameworks, inference engines, and optimization tools will all need to support state-space operations as first-class citizens." — analyst, March 2026
03
Naming is a maze
Nemotron-3 (dense) vs Nemotron 3 (hybrid) vs Llama-Nemotron vs Nemotron-H vs Nemotron Nano 2 …

The Wikipedia entry for Nemotron alone lists: Nemotron-3 8B (dense, 2023), Nemotron-4 15B, Nemotron-4 340B, Llama-Nemotron Nano/Super/Ultra (Llama 3.1 derivatives), Nemotron-H (hybrid), Nemotron Nano 2 — and now Nemotron 3 Nano/Super/Ultra (the new hybrid family, sharing the "Nemotron 3" name with the old dense model). DeepSeek wins on this dimension with three SKUs (V4-Pro / V4-Flash / R3).

A developer searching "best open reasoning model" can't disambiguate which Nemotron is which. Sales conversations stall on "which Nemotron do you mean?"
04
No use-case identity
What is Nemotron for?

DeepSeek owns "frontier reasoning at low cost." Kimi K2.6 owns "coding-agent." Llama owns "the safe enterprise open default." Qwen owns "the multilingual, fine-tunable, Apache-2.0 default." Nemotron has no equivalent one-line claim. "Efficient intelligence on NVIDIA hardware" is true but not motivating — developers care about what they can build, not what runs faster on which chip.

There's grassroots interest where the use-case is clear: r/LocalLLaMA post — "Nemotron Nano 9B classified 3.5M US patents on an RTX 5090" — got 65 upvotes in 2 hours. The model works; the story doesn't carry.

§ 5 · Why this matters more than the leaderboard

It's a chip-company problem. Stated as such.

Jensen and Wall Street are already framing Nemotron as the wedge to drive chip demand. The marketing problem is the moat problem.

Jensen Huang doesn't need a new chip. He needs a new moat.
— CNBC column, March 19, 2026 (post-GTC)
The open-model goal is to be near the frontier rather than absolutely best — Nemotron is fine-tunable infrastructure, a shared open foundation for domain-specific and sovereign AI.
— Paraphrased from Jensen, GTC 2026 (Nemotron Coalition launch)

At GTC 2026, NVIDIA projected $1 trillion in chip orders through 2027 (up from $500B at GTC DC) and announced the Nemotron Coalition — Cursor, LangChain, Mistral, Perplexity, Sarvam, Black Forest Labs — to co-develop Nemotron 4 as "a shared open foundation for domain-specific and sovereign AI." The strategy is named. Every Nemotron adoption is a leading indicator for NVIDIA chip pull-through. Losing the open-model layer means losing the wedge.

§ 6 · Five moves to get from #6 to #1

What the role would execute against.

Each move targets one root cause. Each is measurable. Each can ship within a quarter if it has the right ownership.

MOVE 01
Re-license to Apache 2.0 (or match DeepSeek's MIT)
The single highest-leverage move. The NVIDIA Open Model License is the most-cited adoption blocker in legal-review analyses. Qwen's 90,000-enterprise adoption is because of Apache 2.0. DeepSeek ships MIT. The current license signals "permissive-ish, but governed by California courts" — enterprise poison. This isn't a marketing decision; it's the marketing decision that unlocks everything else.
Targets root cause 01 · Cross-functional: Legal, Exec, Strategy
MOVE 02
Land tooling support before the next launch
Open llama.cpp Mamba-hybrid support is a prerequisite, not a follow-up. Same for MLX (Apple Silicon), Ollama, vLLM, SGLang, HF Transformers. No Nemotron 4 release ships until these are merged. Coordinated by NVIDIA — funded if necessary — with the maintainers. The current state (llama.cpp issue #18064 still open in May 2026) is the smoking gun for why adoption flatlines.
Targets root cause 02 · Cross-functional: Engineering, DevRel, OSS Foundation Eng
MOVE 03
Collapse the naming
One brand: Nemotron. Three sizes: Nano / Super / Ultra. One license. Sunset "Llama-Nemotron," "Nemotron-H," "Nemotron Nano 2," "Nemotron-3 (dense, 2023)." DeepSeek's clarity (V4-Pro / V4-Flash / R3) and Qwen's family naming are textbook. Ship a redirect strategy, refresh every property in 30 days, deprecate the rest.
Targets root cause 03 · Cross-functional: Product Marketing, Web, Docs
MOVE 04
Pick a use-case to dominate. Loss-lead it.
Match the AA throughput signal (220 tok/s, ~3× peers) with a positioning play: "Nemotron — agentic reasoning at frontier throughput, free at launch." 90-day no-rate-limit promo on build.nvidia.com. Cursor, LangChain, Perplexity already in the Coalition — get default-routing in their products. Token volume share IS the marketing. You don't market open models; you ship distribution.
Targets root cause 04 · Cross-functional: Product, Pricing, Coalition partners, DevRel
MOVE 05
DevRel the OpenRouter funnel
Nemotron 3 Super is on OpenRouter's free tier today (openrouter.ai/nvidia/nemotron-3-super-120b-a12b:free) — and invisible in the rankings. Fund a co-marketing push with OpenRouter to drive default-routing for "best open reasoning model." Make build.nvidia.com a one-click experience that mirrors what developers expect from OpenRouter (model card → playground → API key → first call in 60 seconds).
Targets all root causes via distribution · Cross-functional: BD, OpenRouter / Coalition partners
